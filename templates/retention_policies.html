Policy Summary

The BSIT program promotes academic excellence and prepares students for information technology careers. Students must complete these retention standards to stay in good standing.

1

Academic performance

BSIT students must make academic improvement to stay. Failure to satisfy these standards may result in academic probation or program transfer.

1.1 Course failures

Students who fail three or more Major or General Education courses in one semester may be advised to transfer. In extreme instances, the Vice President for Academic Affairs (VPAA) may grant exceptions on the proposal of the Program Chair and College Dean.

1.2 Transfer

Students who fail two or more major courses in one semester may be advised to transfer, using the same approval process.

1.3 General Weighted Average (GWA)

Students must maintain a 3.0 GWA per semester. Except in extraordinary situations, those who fail this requirement may be placed on academic probation for the next semester.

2

Academic probation

Academic probation warns students and gives them information and support to improve. This is meant to guide students who are experiencing academic difficulty.

2.1 Low GWA

GWAs below 3.0 in a semester may place students on academic probation for the next term, except in extreme instances.

2.2 First-time freshmen cases

University freshmen who break academic performance criteria for the first time may be placed on probation instead of being transferred.

2.3 Repeated violations on probation

Students who break academic performance criteria while already on probation may be referred for transfer.

2.4 Lifting probation

VPAA clearance is required for Program Chair and College Dean recommendations to lift probationary status.

3

Academic dismissal

A student who violates any academic policy above while on probation shall be recommended to enroll in other programs or may lead to academic dismissal.

4

Re-enrollment after LOA

After a leave of absence, students must satisfy all return prerequisites before rejoining the program.

4.1 An authorized leave request and proof of compliance with leave criteria.
4.2 Good academic status, as evaluated by the Program Chair and College Dean.

At a glance

  • Maintain at least a 3.0 semester GWA.
  • Avoid repeated failures in major and general education courses.
  • Probation is a warning period, not a permanent status.
  • Violations while on probation can trigger transfer or dismissal.
